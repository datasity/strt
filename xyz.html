Transportation Cost Stress

Analyst Den Research Project

Ogun State, Nigeria March – May 2026 Field Research

Project Overview

Transportation costs are a growing source of economic pressure for commuters and taxi drivers in Ogun State. Rising fuel prices, fare instability, and infrastructure constraints are reshaping daily mobility and household finances. This project documents how transport cost stress affects income, access to work, and daily living.

LocationOgun State, Nigeria
Study PeriodMarch – May 2026
Project TypeField Research
OutputPublic Insights Report

Primary Recipients

  • Ogun State Ministry of Transportation
    Public transport, fares, mobility planning.
  • Ogun State Ministry of Works and Infrastructure
    Road conditions, congestion, route efficiency.
  • Ogun State Ministry of Economic Planning and Budget
    Planning, budgeting, development priorities.

Secondary but Relevant

  • Ogun State Ministry of Local Government and Chieftaincy Affairs
  • Ogun State Traffic Compliance and Enforcement Agency
  • National Bureau of Statistics
  • Federal Ministry of Transportation

Research Objectives

  1. Understand daily commuting costs across key routes
  2. Assess income variability among taxi drivers
  3. Identify peak cost pressure periods
  4. Document commuter coping strategies

Data Collection Methods

  • Structured commuter surveys
  • Taxi driver interviews
  • Peak hour observation studies
  • Income and cost diaries
  • On site video interviews

Survey Coverage

  • Major bus terminals
  • Taxi parks and ranks
  • High traffic corridors
  • Residential to commercial routes

Key Focus Areas

  • Daily commuting expenses
  • Fuel and fare volatility
  • Driver income pressure
  • Time cost of congestion

Expected Deliverables

  • Transportation Cost Stress Index
  • Driver Income Variability Analysis
  • Commuter Cost Profiles
  • Interview video clips
  • Public insights report

Why This Project Matters

Transportation costs directly affect access to work, income stability, and quality of life. This project provides evidence to support better planning, fairer pricing discussions, and informed public dialogue.
